1. Information we collect

When you use CarOtter, the following information may be collected and stored locally on your device:

  • Apple Sign In data: your Apple user ID and, when Apple provides it, your name and email address for account authentication.
  • Vehicle details: make, model, year, VIN, and garage photo information you add.
  • OBD-II data: live readings such as RPM, speed, temperatures, fuel level, readiness information, and diagnostic trouble codes read from a compatible Bluetooth OBD-II adapter.
  • Service and fuel history: service records, odometer values, fuel logs, receipt-derived values, and related notes that you enter or confirm.
  • Chat history: messages exchanged with the in-app assistant.
  • Images you choose to use: receipt photos, vehicle photos, and camera scans used for VIN, odometer, or service-document extraction.
  • Voice input: live microphone audio used to turn speech into text when you use voice input.

2. Permissions and device access

CarOtter asks for device permissions only when a feature needs them:

  • Bluetooth: to discover and connect to a compatible OBD-II adapter.
  • Camera: to scan VIN plates, odometer readings, service documents, fuel receipts, and to take garage vehicle photos.
  • Photo Library: to import saved receipt photos or vehicle photos from your device.
  • Microphone: to capture live audio when you use voice input.
  • Speech Recognition: to convert your speech to text for the chat composer.

3. When data leaves your device

CarOtter is built so most data stays on your device. Data may leave your device only in these situations:

  • AI chat requests: if you enable the AI assistant and provide your own API key, your prompt, chat context, and relevant vehicle context are sent directly from your device to the provider you choose, currently OpenAI or Google Gemini.
  • VIN decoding: when you use VIN lookup, the VIN is sent to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) vPIC service to decode vehicle details.
  • Voice transcription: when you use voice input, audio or transcription data may be processed by Apple's speech recognition services depending on device capability, language support, and iOS behavior. CarOtter does not store raw audio after transcription.
Important: We do not operate our own backend for CarOtter app data. We do not upload your profile, service history, fuel history, or diagnostics to our own servers.

4. How we use information

We use your information only to provide the features you ask the app to perform, including:

  • displaying your vehicle profile, service history, fuel history, and diagnostics,
  • running Bluetooth OBD-II diagnostics and readiness checks,
  • scanning VINs, receipts, odometer readings, and service documents,
  • powering in-app chat responses with the vehicle context you choose to include, and
  • letting you review, export, or delete supported app data.

5. Storage and security

  • Most app data is stored on-device using iOS file protection.
  • Sensitive values such as your AI API key and Apple authentication anchor are stored in the iOS Keychain.
  • OBD debug logs are stored in the device cache area and excluded from backups.
  • We do not use our own cloud database or analytics platform for app data storage.

6. Sharing and selling

We do not sell your personal information. We do not share your personal information for advertising, cross-app tracking, or marketing profiling.

The only third parties that may receive data are the external services you explicitly use through the app, such as your selected AI provider, NHTSA VIN decoding, or Apple's speech recognition services.

7. Your controls

CarOtter includes in-app controls so you can manage your data:

  • Export: from Settings > Your Data, you can export a JSON file containing supported account, service-record, and fuel-log data.
  • Delete by category: you can delete service records, fuel logs, chat history, OBD data, or AI settings individually.
  • Erase all data: you can wipe all app data stored by CarOtter from your device.
  • Sign out: signing out requires re-authentication, but local data is preserved unless you choose to erase it.

8. Children's privacy

CarOtter is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

9. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, we will update this page and revise the "Last updated" date at the top of the document.
